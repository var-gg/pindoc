Why OKLCH. Perceptual uniformity: equal L steps feel equal. A single hue rotation doesn't shift lightness. Matches how Linear, Radix, Tailwind v4 define palettes today.

Why APCA, not 4.5:1. WCAG 2.x overstates contrast of dark colors on light backgrounds and understates mid-tones. APCA (WCAG 3 candidate) measures perceived lightness contrast. Targets: Lc ≥ 60 body text, Lc ≥ 75 small text and labels. All body and UI tokens meet these.

Why light by default. NN/g and MDPI 2024–25 studies show light mode outperforms dark in long-form reading and cognitive tasks in lit environments. The Wiki Reader is long-form; dark remains first-class via toggle.
